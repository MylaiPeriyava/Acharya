This Acharya used to practice a high form of Yoga known as Lambika Yoga. He used to take only dry leaves for food. (It appears He had been in the company of His Acharya for a long time and very shortly (i.e. only after 4 years) after the Siddhi of the previous Acharya, this Acharya also left His physical form. Hence:) During the time of the previous Acharya Shri Brahmanandaghanendra Saraswati, one Kashmiri King by name Lalitaditya had conquered the regions of South Indian also, and coming to Kanchipuram, had submitted himself as a disciple to that previous Acharya. After the Siddhi of the previous Acharya, this Acharya had the king make an Annadana Shala in Kashmir by the Purvashrama Name of His Guru, Jyeshtha Rudra. [This also coincides with "Jyeshtheshvara", the name of the Shiva atop the Gopadri aka Shankaracharya Hill in Shrinagar -- perhaps there is some historical connection.] That Annadana Shala was very famous in that area in those days and was even praised of having made one thousand cookings of rice in one day to feed over a lakh people (perhaps during some major public festival where people congregate in mass numbers). Likewise, during the conquering of the southern regions by Lalitaditya, the then Queen of Karnataka (presumably the King had already passed away) had lost her life. Her son was of young age and inexperienced. This Acharya, out of compassion for the boy, instructed Lalitaditya to put the boy on the throne of Karnataka with appropriate people for guidance and support.

एष ••• लाञ्च्यमेव ॥
Son of Kaṇṇūśaṅkara, the clever Padmanābha by ame, though intent on practising Lambika Yoga, this preceptor Cidānandaghanendra adhering to the injunctions of the Master, remained in the seat of Kāmakoṭi that bestows all desired things to those who resort to, fed innumerable devotees every day.
He, the adept in Yoga reinstating compassionately the Kannada prince who lost kith and kin in his own kingdom through his (the prince's) conqueror, he (Cidānandaghanendra) remained as preceptor only for four years sustaining on dry leaves.
This preceptor reached his Ultimate abode which is devoid of all grief, delusion etc. on the sixty day of bright fortnight in the month of Mārgaśīrṣa of the year Prajotpati.
