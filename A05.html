This Acharya was always meditating on Chandramaulishvara. He was also a great expert in logic, and had composed a commentary by name Chandrika on Shri Sureshvaracharya's work Naishkarmya Siddhi. Due to His great knowledge He was also known as "Jnanottama".

From Punya Shloka Manjari

﻿5. आचार्य - 5 - ज्ञानानन्देन्द्रसरस्वती
जातो ••• सुधीः ॥ १२ ॥
Born of a brahmin called Nāgeśa n the village called Maṅgala of the Chola country, the erudite saint well-known as Śrī Jñānottama, foremost among logicians, having adorned the Pīṭha of preceptors for sixty-three years, attained salvation/siddhi on the seventh day of the bright fortnight in the month of Mārgaśīrṣa of the year Manmatha.
